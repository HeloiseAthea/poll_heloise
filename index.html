"Rate my model"

A tool for scientists and philosophers to think about their animal models, and have conceptual keys to improve them

Rate my model is a tool to obtain an overall quality score for your animal model.
==> The score of each criteria is assigned by yourself (range: -3 to 3)
==> The score of each criteria can be weighted by taking into account your own goals (range: 0 (non applicable) to 3)
==> You can add criteria to take into account the specificity of your model
==> Add comments to justify each attributed score and associated weight

RESULTS: a synthetic document with your inputs, and two rating figures.


3 sections to complete:

  • Qualify your model: by typing short text
  • Plausibility of your model: by rating and typing comments
  • Fiability of associated data: by rating and typing coments
Please run the evaluation for one research question at a time. One evaluation : one question of research, one biological scale.
If your model can be used to study several phenomena, please repeat the evaluation for each of the phenomena.

Plausibility of your model

Homology

Homology evaluates two things: the choice of the species and the choice of a particular strain. This choice will be evaluated according to the function and the type of model previously indicated.
Example: for a biomedical research model aiming at testing a pharmaceutical molecule, a genetic proximity to humans and the selection of a genetic line presenting characteristics of the modeled disease could be favored. Conversely, this phylogenetic proximity and the characteristics of the lineage will not be of much interest for a basic research model (the attributed weight could be close to 0 in thise case).

Face validity

"The face validity of an animal model is the similarity of what is observed in the animal model to what is observed in the human modeled organism."

  • Ethological validity: "similarity of behaviors related to the presumed pathological organism. Meaning matters more than the material similarity."
    • E.g. : "nest building activity of the rodent can be taken as an analogue of the daily activity of the human."
  • Biomarker validity: "similarity of biological markers related to the presume organism. Function matters more than the chemical composition."

Mechanisms

The mechanistic validity of an animal model assesses the similarity of the mechanism we suppose or know is working in the animal (phenomena of interest), to the mechanism that is or is presumed to be working in the human. It refers at the same time to the mechanism that we think is producing the behavior, the biological markers or the symptoms, and to the mechanism we think is sensitive to the action of effective therapeutic agents in case of a disease. This mechanism can be either cognitive or neurobiological."

Predictive validity [study of a disease]

"The predictive validity of an animal model is the similarity of the relation between, on the one hand, the triggering factors and the occurrence of the disease and, on the other hand, between the therapeutic agent and the disease. Roughly speaking, it is the resemblance of the apparent impact of the etiological factors and of the treatment on the observable effects.

  • Induction validity: "the action of the etiological factors on the observable effects of the model disease resemble its action on the observable effects of the human disease."
    "For example, if chronic stress triggers depression-related biomarkers both in humans andin animals, an animal model based on the chronic applicationof stressors may have good induction validity."
  • Remission validity: "the action of the treatment on the observable effects in the animal model may resemble its action on the observable effects in the human disease."
    "For example, if chronic antidepressants induce remission in humans, they should elicit the same effects in the animal model to score high on remission validity."

    • Induction
    Remission

Pathogenic validity

Pathogenic validity of a model assesses the similarity of the processes that lead to disease. We can contrast two kinds of pathognic validity:

  • Ontopathogenic validity: early environmental factors whose interaction with the initial organism produces a vulnerable organism
    E.g. : "maternal care deprivation is generally considered an early environmental factor rendering the subject vulnerable to depressive-like states in adulthood and, thus, has good ontopathogenic validity." "Etiological factors do not have to be materially similar (from a biochemical point of view), but rather semantically similar (what they mean to an organism)."

  • Triggering validity: the similarity of triggering factors occurring during adulthood whose interactions with either a vulnerable or an initial organism produces a pathological organism."
    • E.g : "a cat might elicit fear in rodents, while in humans a white bear might induce the same state. The two stimuli (cat and bear) are not materially identical, but have the same meaning (threat) in the rodents and the humans world."

    Early environmental factor
    Triggering Factors

Internal validity of you experimental system

Reproducibility

To what extent do the animal individuals tested achieve similar results between them?
3: all individuals tested have identical results.
-3: none have identical results.

Fiability

The degree of consistency of a device, an evaluation method, or a test.
A distinction can be made between inter-experimenter reliability: two experimenters or measuring instruments obtain the same result on the same individual.
And inter-tester reliability: the same experimenter obtains the same result by making repeated observations on the same individual.
Evaluate what is the more relevant for your experiment. 3 : all experimenters / all repeated observations on the same individual are the same.

Randomization

Randomly allocating animals among compared conditions.

Convergent validity

Convergent validity aims to evaluate the extent to which the measurement of a particular process, trait or state through a given experimental process correlates with another measurement of the same process, trait or state from another experimental process. Convergent validity seems to be one of the possible ways of taking into account, at the theoretical level, Pierre-Luc Germain's remark concerning the absence of autonomy/self-sufficiency of experimental models.
